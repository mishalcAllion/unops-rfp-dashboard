System 1 of 5 ℹ RFP §2

National Electronic Health Record (NEHR)

Authoritative longitudinal health record for every citizen across all levels of the health system

FHIR R4 HL7
What it does

The NEHR consolidates clinical encounters, laboratory and imaging results, diagnoses, treatments, referrals, and care plans across all levels of the health system. It includes a Patient Access Module — a responsive web application accessible via mobile, tablet, and desktop — enabling both patients and clinicians to access and manage longitudinal health records. ℹ RFP §2 Patient Access

System 2 of 5 ℹ RFP §2

National Health Data Exchange (NHDX) + API Manager

Central interoperability layer — Enterprise Service Bus for all DHP components and legacy systems

ESB / OpenHIE API Gateway
What it does

The NHDX acts as a unified integration hub — enabling secure, standards-based data exchange across all DHP components (NEHR, IdP, Terminology, e-Referral), legacy HIS systems, disease surveillance, and drug distribution. It includes an API management layer for authentication, throttling, monitoring, error handling, logging, and analytics. The vendor must also organize Connectathons — structured integration testing events with legacy HIS vendors. ℹ RFP §2 Connectathons

System 3 of 5 ℹ RFP §2

Identity Provider (IdP)

Authentication and authorization services for all DHP components — Keycloak or similar FOSS

OAuth2 / OIDC FOSS / Keycloak
What it does

Delivers secure, scalable authentication and authorization for all DHP components. Initial focus is on system-level authentication for services connecting to the NHDX. Must be open-source (Keycloak or equivalent) using OAuth2/OpenID Connect. The architecture must also allow future expansion to person-level identity and federation with Sri Lanka's national digital ID system (SLUDI). ℹ RFP §2 Future Scope

System 4 of 5 ℹ RFP §2

Terminology Services + Medication & Device Registry

Centralised management of clinical terminologies (ICD, LOINC, SNOMED, ATC/DDD) with multilingual support

ICD-10/11 LOINC / SNOMED
What it does

Provides centralized management of standardized clinical terminologies, underpinning prescribing, diagnostics, reporting, drug distribution, and analytics. Includes a Medication & Device Registry tracking substances and devices nationally. Must support Sinhala and Tamil translations/transliterations for all public-facing terminology. ℹ RFP §2 Multilingual

🔴
Third-Party Procurement Required

The vendor must procure on MoH's behalf: (1) Drug-drug interaction database (ATC-coded), (2) Drug side-effects database. These are commercial licensed databases — procurement timelines and costs are unpredictable and outside the vendor's control.

System 5 of 5 ℹ RFP §2

e-Referral System + Appointment Management

Structured, traceable referral workflows across preventive, primary, secondary and tertiary care

39 FRs FHIR R4
What it does

Enables electronic referrals from creation through acceptance, scheduling, and completion. Manages appointment sessions (daily/weekly/monthly/ad-hoc), quota allocation per referring unit, and automated patient notifications. Tracks referral status across the full lifecycle. Also provides analytics on referral volumes and service utilisation.
