🔥 Tightest Window

The MVP must cover all 5 systems within 10 weeks of Inception Report acceptance. If MoH takes 1–2 weeks to accept D1, the real build time could be 8–9 weeks. That is the most compressed and highest-risk portion of the entire contract.

⚠ Connectathon Dependency

Connectathons (D3) require coordination with 5 external legacy HIS vendors (HHIMS, HIMS, OpenMRS, Swastha, CloudHIMS). Vendor readiness and documentation quality are entirely outside Allion Tech's control. This can slip D3 and cascade into D4–D8. ℹ

⚠ MoH Acceptance Bottleneck

Every deliverable requires both UNOPS and MoH sign-off before the next phase clock starts. Stakeholder availability and review cycles are outside vendor control. A 2-week review delay on each of 8 deliverables = 16 weeks of unplanned buffer consumed. ℹ

💡 Allion Tech Strategy Note

The bid should include a realistic timeline with buffer allowances for MoH review cycles, SLCERT certification timelines, and Connectathon coordination. Proposing a fully compressed 30-week plan with no slack = high risk of penalties or rejection at later deliverables.
