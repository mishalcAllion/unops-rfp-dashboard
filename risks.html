Timeline Risks
HIGH
ℹ §8 D2

30-Week Timeline is Extremely Compressed

5 interconnected systems — national scale, health-critical — must be designed, built, tested, integrated, and deployed within 30 weeks. The MVP alone covers all 5 systems by week ~14. This is aggressive even for mature teams with established codebases.

Mitigation

Propose a phased MVP with clearly scoped per-system minimums in the Inception Report. Negotiate acceptance timelines with MoH/UNOPS upfront. Staff from day 1 with no ramp-up period.

HIGH
ℹ §1.1

December 2026 Hard External Deadline

The HiQi project funding from Germany's Federal Government expires in December 2026. This is not a soft contractual date — it is driven by an external grant lifecycle. Any delays risk the project being cancelled or the vendor being held liable for the consequences.

Mitigation

Factor the December 2026 hard stop into every schedule assumption. Escalation path to UNOPS must be clear if MoH review cycles cause delays outside vendor control.

Technical Risks
HIGH
ℹ §2 NHDX

Legacy HIS Integration Unknowns

Must integrate with 5 different legacy systems (HHIMS, HIMS, OpenMRS, Swastha, CloudHIMS) — each a different vendor with different APIs, documentation standards, and readiness. The RFP requires Connectathons with all of them. Documentation quality and vendor cooperation are entirely outside Allion Tech's control.

Mitigation

Request pre-bid access to HIS vendor API documentation. Include Connectathon coordination time in the bid plan. Build the adapter layer to be pluggable so one bad integration doesn't block others.

MEDIUM
ℹ §4 Security

SLCERT Certification Process

The vendor must obtain SLCERT (Sri Lanka CERT) certification for the solution. This is a government security certification process — its timeline, criteria, and cost are outside vendor control. If SLCERT has a backlog, this could delay Go-Live.

Mitigation

Begin SLCERT process early — after MVP acceptance. Engage SLCERT before contract start if possible to understand current timelines and requirements.

MEDIUM
ℹ NFN.7

MoH Infrastructure Dependency

All data and compute must reside within MoH's Sri Lanka datacenter (Tier III / Lanka Government Cloud). Vendor deploys onto MoH-provided infrastructure. Speed, reliability, and access provisioning by MoH IT are entirely outside vendor control and could block development.

Mitigation

Negotiate early infrastructure provisioning as a prerequisite to contract start. Include infrastructure readiness as a condition in the Inception Report.

Operational Risks
MEDIUM
ℹ TS.NFN.13

Multilingual Content (Sinhala + Tamil)

All patient-facing content — terminology displays, notifications, UI labels, clinical guides — must be available in Sinhala and Tamil. Medical translation is highly specialized. Finding qualified medical translators for Sinhala and Tamil within the timeline is non-trivial, especially for clinical terminology.

Mitigation

Identify and engage translation resources during bidding. Build translation as a sub-deliverable within D5 scope. Consider using MoH's existing language resources if available.

HIGH
ℹ §2 Terminology

Terminology Database Procurement

The vendor must procure (on MoH's behalf) a drug-drug interaction database and a drug side-effects database with ATC code support. These are commercial, licensed products. Licensing negotiations, procurement cycles, and delivery timelines are unpredictable and could delay the Terminology system.

Mitigation

Identify candidate vendors (e.g., Lexi-Comp, Micromedex, MIMS) before bidding. Include procurement timeline estimates in the bid. Include a contingency scope path if procurement is delayed.

Commercial Risks
HIGH
ℹ §9

Fixed-Price Deliverable Model — No Safety Net

The contract is entirely deliverable-based (milestone fixed-price). All costs — staff, office, equipment, travel, tools, licenses, communication — are 100% the vendor's responsibility. There is no time-and-materials fallback. Cost overruns are fully absorbed by the vendor.

Mitigation

Build comprehensive cost modeling before bidding. Include all overheads (SLCERT, translation, DB procurement, Connectathon coordination) in the financial model. Do not under-price to win.

MEDIUM
ℹ §14 Bond 01

Dual Performance Bonds — 20% of Contract Value

Two performance bonds are required simultaneously: Bond 01 (10% to UNOPS, within 12 working days of award, valid 12 months) and Bond 02 (additional 10% to MoH, valid through the 12-month warranty). This is 20% of total contract value tied up in bank guarantees for up to ~2 years. The issuing bank must have BBB- or better credit rating. ℹ Bond 02

Mitigation

Engage the bank before award to confirm bond issuance capability. Factor bond costs and cash flow impact into financial planning. Confirm the 12-working-day window is achievable.

MEDIUM
ℹ §5

Zero IP Ownership — Strict No Lock-In Clause

All IP vests exclusively with MoH. The vendor retains zero ownership regardless of innovation. Any proprietary technology embedded — even accidentally — constitutes a material breach and grounds for disqualification. Every third-party library must be pre-approved by MoH with written consent. ℹ §5 No Lock-In

Mitigation

Conduct a full technology audit before submission. Document all planned third-party libraries and their licenses. Ensure zero proprietary dependencies in the proposed stack. Open-source preferred by RFP.

LOW
ℹ §10

LKR Currency Exposure

The entire financial proposal must be in Sri Lankan Rupees (LKR). All payments are in LKR. If the firm has USD-denominated costs (e.g., international staff, cloud tools, software licenses), currency fluctuations could affect profitability over the 30-week contract.

Mitigation

Maximize local (Sri Lanka) sourcing to reduce currency exposure. Price any USD-denominated costs with a buffer margin.
